# Framework Scope

**Digital Signal Governance — Documentation**  
Published by Shikhar Jha in association with Michvi LLP | Version 1.1.0

---

## Purpose

This document defines the scope of the Digital Signal Governance (DSG) framework.

Framework scope establishes the governance conditions, domains, and conceptual questions that DSG addresses, as well as the limits of the framework's intended application.

The purpose of scope definition is not to restrict discussion, but to preserve conceptual clarity and maintain consistent framework boundaries.

---

## Scope Statement

Digital Signal Governance (DSG) is concerned with governance conditions affecting digital signals and the environments in which those signals are generated, admitted, interpreted, propagated, and relied upon.

DSG addresses governance conditions that influence the trustworthiness, continuity, legitimacy, interpretation, and downstream reliance of digital signals.

The framework operates at the conceptual governance layer.

---

## In Scope

The following governance domains are within the scope of DSG.

### Signal Governance

Governance conditions affecting signals directly, including:

- Signal Integrity
- Signal Lifecycle
- Signal Admission
- Signal Dependency
- Signal Conflict
- Signal Ambiguity
- Interpretive Integrity

---

### Identity Governance

Governance conditions affecting entity representation across signal environments, including:

- Identity Continuity
- Identity Fragmentation

---

### Consent Governance

Governance conditions affecting authorization states that influence signal legitimacy, including:

- Consent Architecture
- Consent-State Alignment

---

### Measurement Governance

Governance conditions affecting signal-derived measurements, including:

- Measurement Architecture
- Attribution Dependency

---

### Governance Risk

Governance conditions representing aggregate governance exposure arising from signal-layer conditions, including:

- Governance Exposure
- Structural Drift
- Observability Gaps

---

## Governance Questions Within Scope

DSG addresses questions such as:

- What governance conditions exist within a signal environment?
- Are signals governed consistently throughout their lifecycle?
- Are identity representations sufficiently continuous for downstream reliance?
- Are consent states represented and honored consistently?
- Are measurement assumptions documented and governable?
- What governance exposure exists due to degraded, undocumented, or unverifiable conditions?
- Where have governance conditions drifted from their documented state?
- Where do observability limitations prevent governance assurance?

---

## Scope of Assessment

DSG may be used to describe, classify, document, analyze, or reason about governance conditions affecting digital signals.

Such assessment is conceptual and governance-oriented.

DSG does not prescribe how assessments must be performed, what evidence must be collected, what methodologies must be followed, or what operational procedures must be used.

---

## Scope Across Environments

DSG is applicable wherever digital signals are generated, interpreted, propagated, or relied upon for consequential organizational purposes.

Examples may include:

- Measurement environments
- Consent-dependent environments
- Identity-dependent environments
- Reporting environments
- Signal-dependent operational environments
- Multi-system digital environments

These examples are illustrative rather than exhaustive.

---

## Out of Scope

The following areas are outside the scope of DSG as a framework:

### Technical Architecture

DSG does not define:

- Software architectures
- System designs
- Engineering patterns
- Technical configurations
- Infrastructure decisions

---

### Operational Procedures

DSG does not define:

- Operational workflows
- Remediation procedures
- Implementation guides
- Process playbooks
- Deployment methodologies

---

### Audit Methodologies

DSG does not define:

- Audit procedures
- Testing methodologies
- Assurance standards
- Evidence requirements
- Audit frameworks

---

### Compliance Methodologies

DSG does not define:

- Regulatory compliance programs
- Legal interpretations
- Compliance assessments
- Regulatory enforcement procedures

---

### Analytics and Reporting

DSG does not define:

- Analytics methodologies
- Reporting frameworks
- Dashboard structures
- Business intelligence systems
- Attribution models

---

### Technical Detection Systems

DSG does not define:

- Detection algorithms
- Monitoring systems
- Automated controls
- Scoring systems
- Enforcement mechanisms

---

## Scope Discipline

The scope of DSG should be maintained consistently across:

- Terminology development
- Taxonomy expansion
- Boundary documentation
- Research publications
- Future framework revisions

New concepts should be evaluated against DSG scope before admission into the canonical framework.

Concepts that primarily belong to adjacent disciplines should remain outside DSG unless a clear governance justification exists for inclusion.

---

## Relationship to Adjacent Frameworks

DSG may intersect with:

- Design-Time Governance
- Data Governance
- Privacy Governance
- Compliance Frameworks
- Audit Functions
- Analytics Disciplines
- Identity Technologies

Such intersections do not alter DSG scope.

DSG remains focused on governance conditions affecting digital signals and their downstream reliance.

---

## Summary

The scope of DSG is the governance of digital signal conditions.

The framework addresses how signals, identities, consent states, measurements, and governance risks are governed conceptually across digital environments.

DSG does not prescribe implementation, operation, audit, compliance, analytics, or technical mechanisms.

Its purpose is to provide a structured governance vocabulary, taxonomy, and conceptual framework for understanding signal-layer governance conditions.
